Upcoming Disrupt

A disruptive innovation is an innovation that creates a new market and value network and eventually disrupts an existing market and value network, displacing established market leaders and alliances. The term was defined and phenomenon analyzed by Clayton M. Christensen beginning in 1995.

Disrupt Indicators

  • Disruptive innovation must start with a smaller company


    Established companies focus on improving their products and services. In this situation, a smaller, disruptive innovator swoops in and offers a product to those overlooked corporations, which brings us to our next point.

  • Disruptive innovation may originate in a low-end foothold


    The established activities often disregard the disruptive innovators at first because they are so focused on their higher paying clients. This gives the disruptive innovators time to grow and expand and eventually capture a greater client base. By the time the established company discovers what is happening, it is too late to counter. Thus, the disruption theory takes place.

  • Disruptive innovation may originate in a new-market foothold

    New markets typically do not attract well-established businesses because of the strategic work required to get their attention. Disruptive innovators are willing to put in the time and effort to creatively reach out to these new markets and capture the fresh audience.
